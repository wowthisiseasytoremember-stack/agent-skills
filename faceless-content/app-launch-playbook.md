# App Launch Social Media Playbook

## Overview

This playbook covers using social media to launch a software product (app, SaaS, tool). The strategy works whether you have 0 followers or 10,000. It's designed for indie developers and small teams — not VC-backed startups with marketing departments.

---

## The Launch Timeline

### T-minus 12 Weeks: Foundation

**Actions**:
- Set up accounts on 2-3 priority platforms (don't spread across all)
- Define your content pillars (3-4 topics you'll consistently post about)
- Start posting educational/value content in your problem space
- Follow and engage with 50-100 people in your target market

**Content examples**:
- "[Problem your app solves] is costing people [time/money]. Here's why it's harder than it should be."
- Quick tips related to your domain
- Commentary on industry news/trends

**Goal**: Establish presence. Nobody buys from an account that appeared yesterday.

### T-minus 8 Weeks: Build in Public

**Actions**:
- Start sharing development progress
- Show screenshots, design iterations, behind-the-scenes
- Ask your growing audience for input on features/design
- Create a landing page with email capture / waitlist

**Content examples**:
- "Building [app name] - Week 4 update. This week: [feature]. Broke [thing] twice. Here's what I learned."
- Screenshot + "Which design do you prefer? A or B?"
- "I just realized [insight about the problem space]. This changes how I'm building [feature]."

**Goal**: Create investment. People who watch you build something feel ownership of its success.

### T-minus 4 Weeks: Anticipation

**Actions**:
- Increase posting frequency by 50%
- Share sneak peeks, feature demos, beta testimonials
- Start a countdown (subtle, not obnoxious)
- Reach out to niche creators/influencers for potential coverage
- Create your launch assets (announcement posts, demo videos, landing page final version)

**Content examples**:
- Short video demos of key features (15-30s, looping)
- "[X] people on the waitlist. [Personal reflection on what this means to you]."
- "One feature I'm most excited about: [feature]. Here's why I built it."
- DM 20-30 niche creators: "Building [app] for [audience]. Would love your honest feedback if you have 5 minutes."

**Goal**: Maximum anticipation without being annoying about it.

### T-minus 1 Week: Final Prep

**Actions**:
- Schedule all launch day/week posts
- Prepare response templates for common questions
- Brief any collaborators or supporters on launch timing
- Test all links, landing pages, app store listings
- Prepare a "launch thread" or long-form post for your primary platform

**Do NOT**:
- Change anything major in the product this week
- Start new marketing channels you haven't tested
- Assume everything will go smoothly (have a plan B for each platform)

### Launch Day: Go Time

**Hour-by-hour plan**:

```
6:00 AM  — Final check: all links work, app is accessible
7:00 AM  — Post launch announcement on PRIMARY platform
7:15 AM  — Cross-post to all other platforms
7:30 AM  — Start engaging with every comment/reply
8:00 AM  — Share to relevant communities (Reddit, Discord, Slack groups, Product Hunt)
9:00 AM  — Post a follow-up with a different angle (demo video, user story, feature highlight)
12:00 PM — Midday boost post: share any early traction/metrics
3:00 PM  — Afternoon post: answer common questions in public
6:00 PM  — Evening recap: share launch day stats, thank supporters
9:00 PM  — Personal reflection post: "Here's what today felt like"
```

**Launch Day Content Templates**:

Primary announcement:
```
[Punchy one-liner about what it does]

After [X weeks/months] of building, [App Name] is live.

[What it does — one sentence]
[Who it's for — one sentence]
[Link]

I built this because [personal reason — be genuine].

If it helps even one person [outcome], it was worth every late night.

[Thread / details below ↓]
```

Demo video (30-60s):
```
[Screen recording of the app's core workflow]
[Voiceover or text overlay explaining each step]
[End screen: App name + link + "Try it free"]
```

### T-plus 1-4 Weeks: Momentum

**Actions**:
- Share user testimonials and feedback (with permission)
- Post "How to use [feature]" tutorials
- Address common questions/friction points publicly
- Highlight interesting use cases
- Start collecting reviews (app stores, social proof)
- Begin planning your regular content cadence

**Content examples**:
- "Didn't expect this: [user] is using [app] to [unexpected use case]. This is why building in public matters."
- "Most asked question this week: [question]. Here's the answer."
- "[X] users in [Y] days. What's working: [insight]. What I'm fixing: [honest issue]."
- Tutorial: "How to [accomplish key task] in [app] in 60 seconds"

## Platform-Specific Launch Tactics

### Twitter/X Launch
- **Thread format**: 10-15 tweets telling the story of why you built it, what it does, and how to try it
- **Pin the thread** to your profile for the entire launch week
- **Quote-tweet your own thread** with different angles over the following days
- **Reply to your own thread** with link (don't put link in the main thread — algorithm suppresses it)
- **Engage in EVERY reply** for the first 48 hours. Speed of engagement boosts the thread's reach.

### TikTok Launch
- **Problem → Solution video**: Show the pain point (relatable, maybe slightly exaggerated), then the app solving it
- **Before/After**: "How people do [task] now vs. with [app]"
- **Speed demo**: Full workflow in 30 seconds, sped up with key moments highlighted
- **Don't hard-sell on day 1**: TikTok users are allergic to ads. Lead with entertainment/education.

### Instagram Launch
- **Reel**: Similar to TikTok (can reuse content, remove watermark)
- **Carousel**: "I built an app that [does thing]. Here's why and how." Slide-by-slide breakdown.
- **Stories**: Behind-the-scenes, countdown sticker, poll ("Would you use this?"), link sticker
- **Link in bio**: Update to point to your app/landing page

### Reddit Launch
- **Identify 2-3 relevant subreddits** where your target audience hangs out
- **Post as a genuine community member**, not a marketer. "I built this thing, would love feedback" beats "Check out my new app!"
- **Be prepared for brutal honesty** — Reddit will tear apart anything that feels like spam
- **Best subreddits for app launches**: r/SideProject, r/Entrepreneur, r/startups, plus niche-specific subs

### Product Hunt Launch
- **Schedule for Tuesday-Thursday** (highest traffic days)
- **Have 5-10 supporters ready** to upvote and comment within the first hour
- **Prepare a compelling tagline** (≤60 characters) and description
- **Respond to every comment** on your Product Hunt page

## Community-Based Launch Strategy

Beyond social platforms, these communities can drive significant launch traffic:

### Where to Post
- **Hacker News** ("Show HN: [description]") — Tech-savvy audience, very high quality traffic if it hits front page
- **Indie Hackers** — Supportive community for bootstrapped products
- **Relevant Discord servers** — Many niches have active Discords
- **Relevant Slack groups** — Professional communities, especially B2B
- **Relevant Facebook groups** — Still active for many niches, especially non-tech
- **Relevant subreddits** — High potential but strict anti-spam rules

### Community Launch Rules
1. **Be a member before you're a marketer.** Contribute to the community for weeks before your launch.
2. **Lead with value, not promotion.** "I built a free tool that solves [problem]" > "Buy my app!"
3. **Be transparent about what it is.** "I'm the builder" not pretending to be a random user.
4. **Accept feedback gracefully.** Communities will give honest feedback. Thank them for it.
5. **Don't post in more than 2-3 communities on the same day.** It looks spammy.

## Metrics to Track Post-Launch

### Social Metrics (Weekly)
| Metric | Where to Find | What It Tells You |
|--------|--------------|-------------------|
| Follower growth rate | Platform analytics | Is your content attracting your audience? |
| Engagement rate | (likes + comments + shares) / impressions | Is your content resonating? |
| Link clicks | Bio link analytics (Linktree, Bitly) | Is social driving traffic to your app? |
| Best-performing post | Platform analytics | What content type/topic to double down on |

### Product Metrics (Weekly)
| Metric | What It Tells You |
|--------|-------------------|
| Signups from social referrals | Is social actually converting? |
| Activation rate (signed up → used core feature) | Is the product delivering on social's promise? |
| Retention (Day 1, Day 7, Day 30) | Are social-acquired users sticking? |
| NPS / user feedback | What to improve |

### The One Metric That Matters Early
In the first month, focus on **activation rate** — the % of signups who actually complete the core workflow. If this is low, no amount of social media will save you. Fix the product first, then scale marketing.

## Budget Allocation (If Spending Money)

### $0/month (Organic Only)
- All manual posting, all organic growth
- Viable but slower. Expect 3-6 months to meaningful traction.
- Best for: Testing product-market fit before investing in marketing

### $50-100/month
- $0-30 on scheduling tools (Buffer Pro, Later)
- $20-50 on boosted posts / micro-ads (TikTok Promote, Instagram Boost)
- $0-20 on design tools (Canva Pro)
- Best for: Early traction, testing which platforms convert best

### $200-500/month
- $50-100 on ads (targeted at lookalike audiences of competitor users)
- $50-100 on influencer micro-partnerships (send free access, get a post)
- $50 on tools
- $0-250 on content creation (freelancer for thumbnails, graphics)
- Best for: Post-PMF scaling, when you know the product works and need distribution

### Rule of Thumb
Don't spend money on marketing until you have evidence that the product works (users are retaining and getting value). Marketing a product nobody wants is the most expensive way to learn nobody wants it.

## Content Pillar Framework for App Launches

Define 3-4 "pillars" — recurring content themes. Every post should fit one:

**Example for a reselling/inventory app**:

1. **The Problem** (30% of content): Pain points your app solves. "Why manual inventory tracking costs you money." "The 3 hours/week you're wasting on [task]."

2. **The Solution** (25% of content): How your app works. Demos, tutorials, feature highlights. Always tied to a specific benefit, never just "look at this feature."

3. **Domain Expertise** (30% of content): Educational content that positions you as an expert. Tips, strategies, industry insights. This builds trust and attracts your target audience even if they don't need your app yet.

4. **Behind the Scenes** (15% of content): Your journey building the product. Wins, failures, lessons. This humanizes the brand and builds loyalty.

These ratios ensure you're not just pitching your product constantly (which kills engagement) but also not so subtle that nobody knows what you're selling.
