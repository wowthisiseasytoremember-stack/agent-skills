# YouTube SEO Guide

## How YouTube's Algorithm Actually Works (Simplified)

YouTube optimizes for one thing: **watch time on the platform**. Everything else — CTR, AVD, engagement — feeds into that goal. The algorithm doesn't "rank" videos like Google ranks web pages. It RECOMMENDS videos it predicts a specific user will watch.

This means SEO for YouTube is about **matching intent** more than keyword stuffing.

---

## Title Optimization

### Character Count
- **Ideal**: 50-65 characters
- **Maximum visible**: ~70 characters on desktop, ~45 on mobile thumbnails
- **Rule**: Front-load the keyword/hook. Everything after character 45 is bonus.

### Title Formulas (Ranked by Typical CTR)

1. **Number + Superlative + Keyword**: "7 Insane Facts About Deep Sea Creatures" (CTR: 6-10%)
2. **How/Why + Keyword + Twist**: "How NASA Lost $327 Million Over a Math Error" (CTR: 5-9%)
3. **Direct Promise**: "Watch This Before You Buy a Used Car" (CTR: 5-8%)
4. **Curiosity Gap**: "Scientists Found Something Terrifying Under Antarctica" (CTR: 7-12%, but risky if clickbaity)
5. **Vs/Comparison**: "iPhone vs Samsung: I Tested Both for 30 Days" (CTR: 4-7%)

### Title Testing
Generate 5-10 variants. Evaluate each on:
- Does it create curiosity? (Would YOU click this?)
- Does it include the target keyword naturally?
- Is the most important word in the first 5 words?
- Does it avoid clickbait that the video can't deliver on? (Broken promises = dislike ratio + low AVD)

### Words That Boost CTR
Actually, Secret, Truth, Real Reason, Nobody Talks About, Finally, Shocking, Insane, Surprising, What Happens When, I Tested, Exposed

### Words That Tank CTR
Part 1, Episode, Update, Vlog, My Thoughts On (unless you're already famous)

## Description Optimization

### Structure
```
[Line 1-2: Hook/value prop — this shows in search results and above the fold]

[Timestamps]
0:00 Introduction
1:23 [Section with keyword]
4:56 [Section with keyword]
...

[Body: 200-500 words of natural, keyword-rich prose]
This video covers [primary keyword] and explores [secondary keyword]...

[Links section]
Related videos: [links]
Sources: [links — this builds trust AND helps YouTube understand your content]

[Hashtags: 3-5 max]
#primarykeyword #secondarykeyword #nichekeyword
```

### Description Rules
- **First 150 characters matter most.** They show in search results. Make them count.
- **Include your target keyword in the first sentence.**
- **Timestamps are NOT optional.** They improve watch time (viewers jump to sections instead of leaving), help YouTube understand content structure, and can appear as "key moments" in search.
- **Don't keyword stuff.** YouTube's NLP is sophisticated. Write naturally with keywords woven in.
- **Link to your other videos.** Creates a content web that boosts session time.

## Tags Strategy

Tags are less important than they used to be, but still worth doing. YouTube uses them mainly for spelling/context disambiguation.

### Tag Structure (15-30 tags)
```
Tier 1 (3-5): Exact match keywords
  "how bitcoin mining works"
  "bitcoin mining explained"

Tier 2 (5-10): Broad topic keywords
  "bitcoin"
  "cryptocurrency"
  "crypto mining"
  "blockchain"

Tier 3 (3-5): Related topics
  "passive income"
  "crypto for beginners"
  "digital currency"

Tier 4 (3-5): Competitor/related channel names
  "whiteboard crypto"
  "coin bureau"
```

### Tag Rules
- First tag = exact target keyword
- Include common misspellings if relevant
- Include both singular and plural forms
- Total character limit: 500 characters across all tags

## Thumbnail Strategy for Faceless Channels

Without a face, thumbnails need to work harder. The three elements that work:

### 1. Bold Text (3-5 Words)
- Font: Thick, sans-serif (Impact, Bebas Neue, Anton)
- Size: Large enough to read on a phone
- Contrast: White/yellow text on dark background, or dark text on bright background
- Position: Left or center (YouTube overlays timestamp on bottom-right)

### 2. Dominant Visual Element
- One clear subject, not a collage
- High saturation / high contrast
- Faces (even illustrated) get 30%+ higher CTR than objects
- Arrows, circles, or red X marks draw attention

### 3. Emotion/Intrigue
- Something unexpected, dramatic, or intriguing
- Before/after comparisons
- Scale comparison (tiny thing next to huge thing)
- Visual contradiction (luxury item in a garbage dump)

### Thumbnail Specs
- **Resolution**: 1280 x 720 pixels (16:9)
- **File size**: Under 2MB
- **Format**: JPG or PNG
- **Safe zone**: Keep key elements away from edges (YouTube rounds corners on mobile)

### A/B Testing
YouTube now offers native thumbnail A/B testing (rolled out 2024). Use it. Small CTR improvements compound over thousands of impressions.

## Shorts Strategy

YouTube Shorts can be a powerful growth lever for the main channel.

### How to Use Shorts for Growth
1. Take a 30-60 second clip from a long-form video (the hook + one compelling point)
2. Reformat to 9:16 vertical
3. Add captions (mandatory for Shorts — many viewers watch muted)
4. End card: "Full video on our channel"

### Shorts SEO
- Title: 40 characters max (Shorts titles are truncated aggressively)
- Description: Less important, but include 2-3 hashtags including #Shorts
- First frame matters — it's the "thumbnail" in the Shorts feed

### Shorts Monetization
- $0.01-0.07 per 1,000 views (much lower than long-form)
- Main value: subscriber acquisition, not direct revenue
- 10M Shorts views in 90 days = alternative path to YPP

## Analytics Benchmarks

### What "Good" Looks Like

| Metric | Poor | Average | Good | Excellent |
|--------|------|---------|------|-----------|
| CTR | <2% | 2-4% | 4-8% | 8%+ |
| AVD (% of video) | <25% | 25-40% | 40-60% | 60%+ |
| Like ratio | <2% | 2-4% | 4-6% | 6%+ |
| Sub conversion | <0.5% | 0.5-1% | 1-3% | 3%+ |
| Comment rate | <0.1% | 0.1-0.5% | 0.5-1% | 1%+ |

### Diagnostic Framework
- **Low CTR + High AVD**: Great content, bad packaging. Fix title/thumbnail.
- **High CTR + Low AVD**: Good packaging, content doesn't deliver. Fix script/pacing.
- **Low CTR + Low AVD**: Both need work. Start with the script (foundation).
- **High CTR + High AVD**: Working. Make more content like this.

### Growth Expectations (Faceless Channels)
- **Month 1-3**: 0-500 subscribers. Expect near-zero traction. This is normal.
- **Month 3-6**: 500-2,000 subscribers if uploading 2-3x/week with decent SEO.
- **Month 6-12**: 2,000-10,000 if content quality and consistency hold.
- **Month 12+**: Compounding begins. Older videos start generating sustained traffic.

The first video to "pop" (get recommended by the algorithm) usually happens between video 20-50. Before that, almost all traffic is search-based.

## Content Calendar Strategy

### Pillar Content (60% of uploads)
Evergreen explainers, listicles, and deep dives. These compound over time via search.

### Trending Content (30% of uploads)
Timely takes on news, trends, or events in your niche. Higher initial views, shorter shelf life.

### Experimental Content (10% of uploads)
Different formats, lengths, or angles. This is how you discover what works.

### Frequency
- **Minimum viable**: 1x/week
- **Growth mode**: 2-3x/week
- **Burnout prevention**: Batch produce (script 5 videos → record 5 → edit 5) rather than one-at-a-time

Consistency matters more than frequency. 1x/week for 52 weeks beats 5x/week for 4 weeks then silence.
